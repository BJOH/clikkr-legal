Clikkr — Privacy Policy

Effective 22 July 2026

Clikkr is a counting app. It is built so that your data stays yours: there are no accounts, no advertising, no tracking, and no third-party analytics. We (the developer) cannot see your counters or anything you record with them.

What the app stores

Your counters, counts, goals, and any notes or photos you attach to a count are stored in the app's database on your device. If you are signed into iCloud, this data also syncs through your private iCloud database (Apple CloudKit) so it follows you across your devices. That database belongs to your Apple account — the developer has no access to its contents. Deleting a counter deletes its counts, notes, and photos.

Location

Counters can optionally stamp counts with your location, so you can see where you counted on a map. This is off until you enable it, uses the "While Using the App" permission level, and can be turned off per counter or revoked in Settings at any time. Location stamps are stored with your counts on your device and in your private iCloud database — they are never sent to the developer or anyone else.

Photos

If you attach a photo to a count — from your library, your camera, or the "photos taken here" suggestions — the image is stored with your data as described above. Library access uses the iOS picker, which shares only the photos you choose.

Notifications

Goal-reached notifications are generated locally on your device. Nothing about them leaves it.

Diagnostics

The app records aggregate performance and crash diagnostics via Apple's MetricKit, stored locally in the app's own container. Separately, if you have opted into "Share with App Developers" in iOS Settings, Apple may share standard anonymized crash reports with the developer. The app contains no other analytics or telemetry.

Purchases

The Clikkr+ subscription is processed entirely by Apple through the App Store. The developer never sees your payment details.

Your rights

Since all data lives on your devices and in your private iCloud, you control it directly: delete data in the app, or delete the app to remove its local data. iCloud data can be managed via iOS Settings → your Apple account → iCloud. Under GDPR you also have rights of access, rectification and erasure — which in Clikkr's design you exercise yourself, since the developer holds no copy of your data.
